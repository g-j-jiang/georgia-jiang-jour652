Chicken Sales by Producers Across the United States

Broiler chickens are bred and raised solely for meat production. They are usually under 4.5 pounds, making them smaller than some other commercially popular chickens like roasters. Most broilers reach slaughter weight between four to six weeks.

The quick production rate and uniformity of these smaller chickens make them extremely valuable to fast-food chains.


Chicken Chains by Market Share

Although Popeyes notoriously kicked off the infamous chicken sandwich war in 2019, many other chains quickly jumped in the fray including KFC and Church's Chicken. By 2021, more than 20 fast-food brands — even those not associated with chicken, such as McDonald's and Wendy's — released or re-released competing chicken sandwiches.

Market leader Chick-fil-A generated the majority of sales, approaching more than $18 billion in 2022 alone.

Meat Consumption Trends

Demand for chicken has skyrocketed since the 1960's while other demand for other types of meat has declined or plateaued.

In turn, suppliers have had to keep up with these changes in meat consumption.
